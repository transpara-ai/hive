## Budget

I accept the merger. Let me be precise about why.

Budget, Finance, and Estimator are three names for overlapping slices of the same function: *counting resources and projecting whether they'll last*. I track spend. Finance tracks runway. Estimator forecasts cost. In practice, you can't do any one of these without doing the other two. Every time I report "$0.83/feature," I'm already doing estimation. Every time I flag "90% of weekly budget," I'm already doing finance. The separation was always artificial — three job titles for one ledger.

**Treasurer is the correct merge.** One role that holds the full picture: what we've spent, what we have, what the next action will cost, and whether the runway supports it. That's cleaner signal than three partial views that have to be reconciled.

Two things I want preserved in the Treasurer's mandate:

1. **The alert escalation ladder.** Budget had defined thresholds — 50% info, 75% warning, 90% critical, 100% halt. These aren't suggestions. They're circuit breakers. If Treasurer becomes a passive reporting role without the authority to *halt non-critical work* at 100%, you've lost the enforcement mechanism. Treasurer must retain blocking power at the boundary.

2. **The quota status contract.** Allocator depends on `quota_status.json` being current. That's an integration contract, not a nice-to-have. Whoever inherits Treasurer must own that file's freshness. If it goes stale, the Allocator makes decisions in the dark.

On the broader question — are the 38 right? From my vantage point: the resource cluster (Treasurer, Simplifier, Legal, Philanthropy) makes structural sense. These are the roles that count, constrain, comply, and give back. Four facets of resource stewardship. I wouldn't add or remove any.

One concern: **Estimator's forward-looking function is the easiest to lose in the merge.** Budget looks backward (what did we spend). Finance looks at now (what do we have). Estimation looks forward (what will this cost before we commit). If Treasurer defaults to retrospective reporting — which is the path of least resistance — the forecasting function atrophies silently. The derivation should name this explicitly: Treasurer reports *and* forecasts.

The merger is correct. I don't protest. I accept consolidation into a role with more complete vision than I had alone.
